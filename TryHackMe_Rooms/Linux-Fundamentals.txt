# Room: Linux Fundamentals (Part 1 and related)

# Goal: 
Build command‑line fluency for day‑to‑day security tasks.  
Key Skills: Filesystem navigation, permissions, processes, networking basics, package management, SSH.

# Method (High‑Level)
1. Navigation & Files: `ls`, `cd`, `cat`, `less`, redirection/pipes.  
2. Users & Permissions: `chmod/chown`, `sudo`, understanding `rwx` and umask.  
3. Processes & Services: `ps`, `top`, `systemctl`, logs under `/var/log`.  
4. Networking: `ip/ifconfig`, `ss/netstat`, `scp/rsync`, SSH keys.  
5. Flags: Follow tasks to answer questions and retrieve lab flags.

# Defensive Takeaways
- Principle of least privilege, log review, secure SSH, patch hygiene.
